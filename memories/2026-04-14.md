## Translation gotcha — "稿" is narrowly manuscript-scoped

[2026-04-14] When translating general-purpose "rewrite / revision / draft iteration" from English to Chinese, do NOT use "稿 + verb" constructions (e.g. "一稿一稿地改", "改稿", "重写稿") unless the essay is specifically about writing articles. 稿 in Chinese defaults to *manuscript/article-drafting*, which narrows the metaphor and breaks essays that span research, tool-building, or cross-domain work. Prefer object-free iteration phrases like "一次比一次更清楚一点" or "一遍一遍地磨", or echo the callback target directly (e.g. "push back, 重写" in the body). Surfaced while translating *It All Comes Down to Taste* — the closing "one rewrite at a time" was originally rendered as "一稿一稿地改" and the author flagged it as scope-wrong.
